Sir Isaac Newton

Biography

Sir Isaac Newton (1642–1727) was an English mathematician, physicist, astronomer, and author widely recognized as one of the most influential scientists of all time. He formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation, which became the foundation for classical mechanics. His book *Philosophiæ Naturalis Principia Mathematica* ("Mathematical Principles of Natural Philosophy"), published in 1687, is regarded as one of the most important works in the history of science.

Major Contributions

  • Formulated the three laws of motion.
  • Discovered the law of universal gravitation.
  • Developed calculus (independently of Leibniz).
  • Conducted groundbreaking work in optics, including the study of light and color.

Legacy

Newton's discoveries have profoundly influenced science and mathematics. His work laid the groundwork for centuries of scientific thought and remains a cornerstone of physics today.
